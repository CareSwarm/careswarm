# Fever in Children — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common pediatric guidance (WHO IMCI-style) for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Age changes everything
- **Under 3 months with ANY fever ≥38.0°C: see a doctor the same day — always.** Young infants can deteriorate quickly and signs are subtle.
- 3–36 months: assess behavior over the number. A playful, drinking, interactive child with 38.5°C is usually less concerning than a listless child with 38.0°C.

## Home care
Offer fluids frequently (breastfeeds count); light clothing; room ~24°C. Paracetamol 10–15 mg/kg every 4–6h (max 4 doses/day) or ibuprofen 5–10 mg/kg every 6–8h (over 3 months, weight-dosed, with food). **Never aspirin in children** (Reye's syndrome). Dose by weight, not age, using the syringe provided.

## See a doctor within 24 hours
- Fever lasting more than 3 days at any age.
- Ear pulling/pain, painful urination, refusing fluids, fewer wet diapers.
- Fever returning after a clear day, or parents are worried — parental instinct is a validated warning sign.

## Emergency criteria (go now / call emergency services)
- Any fever under 3 months of age.
- Non-blanching rash (does not fade when pressed with a glass).
- Stiff neck, bulging fontanelle, persistent vomiting.
- Breathing hard: ribs sucking in, grunting, nostrils flaring, too breathless to feed.
- Drowsy, floppy, hard to wake; high-pitched or weak cry.
- Febrile seizure lasting >5 minutes (any first seizure should be assessed same day).
- Signs of dehydration: sunken eyes, no tears, dry mouth, no urine for 8+ hours.
