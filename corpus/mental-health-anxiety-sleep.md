# Anxiety, Low Mood and Sleep — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common mental-health first-line guidance (WHO mhGAP-style) for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Normal stress vs needing help
Worry that is intermittent and proportionate to events is normal. Seek professional support when symptoms persist most days for 2+ weeks AND interfere with work, study, or relationships: constant worry, irritability, poor concentration, loss of interest in enjoyable things, hopelessness, appetite or sleep change.

## Evidence-based self-help (effective for mild symptoms)
- **Behavioral activation**: schedule one small pleasant or meaningful activity daily; action precedes motivation, not the other way around.
- **Slow breathing**: 4 seconds in, 6 seconds out, 5 minutes, twice daily — measurably lowers acute anxiety.
- **Movement**: 30 minutes brisk walking 5×/week performs comparably to medication for mild depression.
- **Limit alcohol and late caffeine** — both are anxiety amplifiers and sleep destroyers.
- Stay connected: isolation feeds both anxiety and low mood; one real conversation daily.

## Sleep hygiene (insomnia first-line)
Fixed wake time 7 days/week (the anchor); bed only when sleepy; out of bed after ~20 minutes awake; screens off 1 hour before bed; bedroom dark, quiet, cool; no naps after 3pm; caffeine before noon only. Sleeping pills are a short-term bridge at most — CBT-I is the durable treatment.

## Seek professional care promptly
Symptoms >2 weeks despite self-help; panic attacks recurring; trauma symptoms (flashbacks, nightmares); any psychotic symptoms (hearing voices, paranoia) — urgent review.

## Emergency
Thoughts of suicide or self-harm with any plan or intent: do not stay alone — contact emergency services (115 in Vietnam) or a crisis hotline immediately; remove means; stay with the person until help arrives.
