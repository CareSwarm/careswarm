# Type 2 Diabetes — Daily Management Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common ADA/WHO-style guidance for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Diagnosis thresholds
Fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL), HbA1c ≥6.5%, or random glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL) with symptoms (thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss). Prediabetes: fasting 5.6–6.9 mmol/L — the window where lifestyle change is most powerful.

## Targets (typical, individualized by the doctor)
- HbA1c: <7% for most adults; looser (<8%) for frail elderly to avoid hypoglycemia.
- Fasting: 4.4–7.2 mmol/L (80–130 mg/dL); post-meal (2h): <10 mmol/L (180 mg/dL).
- Also control blood pressure (<130/80) and cholesterol — cardiovascular disease is the main killer in diabetes.

## Daily management
- Plate method: half non-starchy vegetables, quarter lean protein, quarter whole-grain carbs. White rice portions matter more than sugar in tea.
- 150 min/week activity + resistance training twice weekly; walk 10–15 minutes after the largest meal.
- Metformin: take with food; mild stomach upset usually settles in 1–2 weeks; report persistent diarrhea.
- Annual checks: eyes (retinopathy), feet (sensation, ulcers), kidneys (urine albumin), dental.

## Hypoglycemia (if on insulin/sulfonylureas)
Symptoms: shaking, sweating, hunger, confusion. Rule of 15: take 15g fast sugar (3 teaspoons sugar or half a glass of juice), recheck in 15 minutes, repeat if still <4.0 mmol/L (70 mg/dL); then eat a snack with carbs + protein.

## Urgent/emergency
- Glucose >16.7 mmol/L (300) persistently with vomiting, deep rapid breathing, fruity breath, or drowsiness → emergency (possible DKA/HHS).
- Severe hypoglycemia with confusion or unconsciousness → emergency; give glucagon if trained, do NOT pour liquids into an unconscious person's mouth.
- Foot wound that is red, warm, spreading, or not healing in 3 days → urgent review (amputation prevention).
