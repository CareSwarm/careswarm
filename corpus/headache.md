# Headache — Assessment and Self-Care Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common primary-care guidance for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Common benign patterns
- **Tension-type headache**: band-like pressure both sides, builds through the day, linked to stress, screens, poor sleep, neck strain. Usually responds to rest, hydration, simple analgesics (paracetamol/acetaminophen per label), and regular breaks.
- **Migraine**: throbbing, often one-sided, with nausea or sensitivity to light/sound, lasting 4–72 hours; may have visual aura. Rest in a dark quiet room; early simple analgesia; identify triggers (sleep change, skipped meals, certain foods, hormonal cycle).
- **Medication-overuse headache**: daily or near-daily headache in people using pain relievers >10–15 days per month — gradual withdrawal under guidance is the treatment.

## When to see a doctor (within days)
- New headaches after age 50.
- Headaches increasing in frequency or severity over weeks.
- Headache with persistent visual change, weakness, or numbness.
- Headaches that wake the person from sleep or are worse on coughing/straining.
- Mild headache persisting more than 7–10 days despite self-care.

## Emergency criteria (call emergency services)
- **Thunderclap headache**: sudden, "worst headache of my life", peaking within a minute.
- Headache with fever AND stiff neck or rash (possible meningitis).
- Headache after significant head injury, with confusion, vomiting, or drowsiness.
- Headache with stroke signs (face droop, arm weakness, slurred speech).

## Self-care basics
Regular sleep schedule, hydration (1.5–2L/day), limit caffeine to morning, 20-20-20 screen breaks, gentle neck stretches. Keep a simple headache diary (time, triggers, relief) — it shortens the path to diagnosis.
