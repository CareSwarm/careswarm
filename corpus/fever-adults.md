# Fever in Adults — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common primary-care guidance for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Definitions
Fever: body temperature ≥38.0°C (100.4°F). Low-grade 38.0–38.9°C; high ≥39.0°C. Measure with a reliable thermometer; ear/forehead readings vary.

## Most fevers are viral
Typical course: 2–4 days alongside cough, sore throat, runny nose, muscle aches. Management: rest, fluids (2–3L/day unless restricted), paracetamol or ibuprofen per package directions for comfort — treat discomfort, not the number.

## See a doctor within 24–48 hours if
- Fever persists beyond 3 days, or returns after a clear day.
- Localizing symptoms: painful urination (UTI), one-sided facial pain (sinusitis), productive cough with chest pain (possible pneumonia), severe sore throat with white patches.
- Recent travel to a malaria/dengue region — same-day testing recommended.
- Chronic conditions: diabetes, heart/lung disease, kidney disease, pregnancy.
- Age over 65 — lower threshold for review.

## Emergency criteria
- Fever with stiff neck, severe headache, or new rash that does not fade under pressure.
- Fever with confusion, difficulty breathing, chest pain, or fainting.
- Fever ≥40°C not responding to antipyretics.
- Fever in anyone immunosuppressed (chemotherapy, transplant, untreated HIV) — treat as urgent.
- Signs of sepsis: very fast breathing/heartbeat, mottled or cold skin, no urination, extreme drowsiness.

## What not to do
No antibiotics without prescription — they do nothing for viral illness and breed resistance. Avoid alcohol sponging or ice baths. Do not combine multiple products containing the same active ingredient (accidental paracetamol overdose risk).
