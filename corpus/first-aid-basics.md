# First Aid Basics — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of standard first-aid guidance (Red Cross-style) for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Burns
Cool running water 20 minutes (not ice, not toothpaste, not fish sauce). Remove rings/watches before swelling. Cover loosely with clean non-stick dressing. Seek care for: burns larger than the person's palm, on face/hands/joints/genitals, blistering deeply, electrical or chemical burns, any burn in infants.

## Cuts and bleeding
Firm direct pressure with clean cloth 10 minutes without peeking. Elevate the limb. Once stopped: clean with running water, antiseptic, dressing. Stitches likely needed if edges gape, deeper than 5mm, or over a joint — within 8–12 hours. Tetanus booster if wound is dirty and last dose >5 years.

## Choking (adult/child over 1 year)
Encourage coughing first. If silent/can't breathe: 5 sharp back blows between shoulder blades, then 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich); alternate. Unconscious → start CPR. Infants under 1: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts, never abdominal.

## Sprains (RICE, first 48h)
Rest, Ice 15–20 min every 2–3h (cloth-wrapped), Compression bandage, Elevation. See a doctor if: can't bear weight 4 steps, bone tenderness, joint looks deformed, or numbness.

## Fainting
Lay flat, raise legs 30cm, loosen tight clothing, fresh air. Recovery within a minute is typical. Seek care if: fainting during exercise, with chest pain/palpitations, no warning signs beforehand, or injuries from the fall — cardiac causes must be excluded.

## Nosebleed
Sit LEANING FORWARD, pinch the soft part of the nose 10–15 minutes continuously. Not backward (swallowing blood causes vomiting). Emergency if >30 minutes, after head injury, or on blood thinners with heavy flow.
