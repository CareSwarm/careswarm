# Emergency Red Flags — When to Call Emergency Services

Educational summary of widely accepted emergency-care criteria (WHO/Red Cross-style guidance) for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Call emergency services immediately for
- **Chest pain** lasting more than 15 minutes at rest, or with sweating, nausea, breathlessness, radiation to jaw or left arm, or collapse.
- **Breathing difficulty**: unable to speak full sentences, blue lips, gasping, severe wheeze not responding to usual inhaler.
- **Stroke signs (FAST)**: Face drooping on one side, Arm weakness, Speech slurred or strange — Time to call immediately. Sudden severe headache ("worst of life"), sudden vision loss, or sudden one-sided numbness count too.
- **Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness**, new confusion, or seizures (first seizure, or lasting >5 minutes).
- **Severe bleeding** that does not stop with 10 minutes of firm direct pressure; vomiting blood; black tarry stools with faintness.
- **Anaphylaxis**: swelling of lips/tongue/throat, widespread hives with breathing trouble or dizziness after food, medication, or sting. Use an epinephrine auto-injector if available, then call.
- **Severe dehydration** in infants/elderly: no urination, sunken eyes, lethargy.
- **Major trauma**: falls from height, high-speed accidents, suspected spinal injury (do not move the person).
- **Thoughts of suicide or self-harm with a plan** — stay with the person and contact emergency services or a crisis line immediately.

## While waiting for help
Keep the person still and warm; do not give food or drink; if unconscious and breathing, place in the recovery position; if not breathing, begin CPR if trained.

## Vietnam emergency number
115 (medical). In other countries use the local equivalent (911 US, 112 EU).
