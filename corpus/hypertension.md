# High Blood Pressure (Hypertension) — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common WHO/ISH-style guidance for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Thresholds (clinic measurements, repeated)
- Normal: <120/80 mmHg. Elevated: 120–139/80–89.
- Hypertension: ≥140/90 on two or more separate days. Home readings ≥135/85 count.
- One high reading is not a diagnosis — confirm with repeated, rested measurements (seated 5 minutes, arm supported, no caffeine/smoking 30 minutes prior).

## Why it matters
Untreated hypertension silently drives heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, and dementia. It typically has NO symptoms — "feeling fine" is not evidence of control.

## Lifestyle measures (first-line, proven)
- Salt under 5g/day (≈1 teaspoon total, including sauces — fish sauce and soy sauce are major hidden sources).
- 150 minutes/week moderate exercise (brisk walking counts).
- Weight: every 1kg lost ≈ 1 mmHg reduction.
- Limit alcohol (≤2 standard drinks/day men, ≤1 women); stop smoking.
- DASH-style diet: vegetables, fruit, whole grains, less processed food.

## Medication notes
Common first-line classes: ACE inhibitors/ARBs, calcium-channel blockers, thiazide diuretics. Take at the same time daily; do not stop because readings normalized — they normalized because of the medication. Report ankle swelling, persistent cough, or dizziness to the prescriber rather than self-stopping.

## Seek urgent care
- Readings ≥180/120 with headache, chest pain, visual change, breathlessness, or confusion → emergency (hypertensive crisis).
- Readings ≥180/120 without symptoms → recheck after 5 quiet minutes; if persistent, same-day medical review.
