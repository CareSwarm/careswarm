# Preventive Health Screening for Adults — Guideline Summary

Educational summary of common WHO/USPSTF-style screening guidance for the CareSwarm local corpus.

## Every adult, regardless of age
- Blood pressure: at least yearly (any pharmacy visit counts).
- Weight/BMI and waist circumference: yearly.
- Dental check: every 6–12 months. Eye exam: every 1–2 years after 40.
- Vaccination upkeep: tetanus booster every 10 years; annual influenza; hepatitis B if not immune (high prevalence in Vietnam — worth checking status).

## From age 35–40
- Fasting glucose or HbA1c every 3 years (yearly if overweight or family history) — type 2 diabetes is silent for years.
- Lipid panel (cholesterol) every 4–6 years; more often if abnormal or cardiovascular risk.

## From age 45–50
- **Colorectal cancer**: stool-based test (FIT) yearly or colonoscopy every 10 years, from age 45.
- **Breast cancer**: mammography every 1–2 years from 40–50 per local program; clinical breast exam awareness at any age.
- **Cervical cancer**: Pap smear every 3 years (21–65) or HPV test every 5 years (30–65) — the single highest-yield cancer screen.
- **Liver cancer surveillance** (ultrasound + AFP every 6 months) for chronic hepatitis B/C carriers — particularly relevant in Southeast Asia.

## From age 50+
- Osteoporosis: bone density for women 65+, earlier with risk factors (fracture, steroids, smoking).
- Abdominal aortic aneurysm: one-time ultrasound for men 65–75 who ever smoked.
- Hearing check if conversations are hard to follow.

## The honest caveat
Screening finds disease early in people who feel WELL. Symptoms (lumps, bleeding, weight loss, persistent pain) are NOT screening territory — they need diagnostic evaluation now, whatever the last screen said.
